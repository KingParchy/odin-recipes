Ackee and Saltfish

Home

Description

Widely regarded as the national dish of Jamaica, Ackee and Saltfish is a masterful balance of savory, salty, and delicate flavors. The star of the dish is the ackee, a tropical fruit that, when cooked, takes on a soft, creamy consistency similar to scrambled eggs. It is sautéed with flaked salt cod (which has been boiled down to remove excess salt), Scotch bonnet peppers, onions, and tomatoes. This combination creates a rich, umami-forward breakfast or brunch staple that is deeply embedded in Caribbean culture.

The dish is rarely eaten alone and is traditionally served with "hard food" or fried accompaniments. As seen here, the meal is rounded out by fried dumplings—dense, golden-brown dough balls—and slices of ripe fried plantain. The sweetness of the caramelized plantains provides a perfect counterpoint to the saltiness of the fish, while the dumplings serve as the ideal vessel for soaking up the flavorful oils and spices from the main pan.

Ingredients

  • The Protein: 500g (approx. 1 lb) Salted Codfish
  • The Fruit: 24 fresh Ackee pods (carefully cleaned) or 350g canned Ackee
  • The Render: 6 strips of bacon, sliced into small bits
  • Aromatics: 1 medium yellow onion (diced), 2 scallions (sliced), and 1 Scotch Bonnet chili (minced and deseeded)
  • Fresh Addition: 1 vine-ripened tomato, chopped
  • Seasoning: Freshly ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Hydrate the Fish: Begin by soaking the salted cod in cold water for 30 minutes to soften. Drain, then transfer to a pot with fresh water and bring to a rolling boil. Once the fish is tender, drain it and use a fork to flake the meat into small pieces, discarding any skin or bones. Set aside.
  2. Ackee Prep: If using fresh fruit, extract the "pegs" from the pods. Remove the seeds and the red/pink membranes entirely. Briefly parboil the ackee in salted water until just tender, then drain immediately. If using canned, simply drain and rinse gently to keep the fruit intact.
  3. Build the Flavor: In a large skillet over medium heat, fry the bacon until the fat renders and it becomes crispy. Remove the excess fat, then toss in the onion, scallions, and Scotch Bonnet. Sauté until the vegetables are translucent and aromatic.
  4. Combine and Finish: Carefully fold in the flaked cod, prepared ackee, and diced tomato. Season with black pepper and allow the mixture to simmer over low heat for roughly 5 minutes so the flavors can integrate.
  5. Service: Use a slotted spoon to plate the dish, ensuring excess oil stays in the pan. Accompany with traditional "festival" or golden fried dumplings.
