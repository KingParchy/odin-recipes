Description

Jamaican "Festivals" are a beloved staple of island street food, most famous for being the essential companion to fried fish or jerk pork. Unlike a standard savory dumpling, a festival is defined by its distinct oblong shape and a subtle sweetness that comes from the addition of sugar and cornmeal. This sweetness serves a functional purpose, acting as a cooling counterpoint to the intense heat of Scotch bonnet-based dishes.

The texture is the true hallmark of a well-made festival: the exterior should be crispy and golden-brown, while the inside remains soft and slightly dense. As shown in the photo, they are traditionally served hot and fresh, often resting on parchment paper to soak up the residual oil from the deep-frying process. They are simple to make but remain one of the most comforting elements of a traditional Jamaican "spread."

Ingredients

  • The Grain: 1 cup (150g) yellow cornmeal (fine-ground works best)
  • The Structure: 3/4 cup (100g) all-purpose flour
  • Sweetener: 1/4 cup (60g) light brown sugar, firmly packed
  • Rising Agent: 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Seasoning: 1/2 teaspoon fine salt
  • Binding Agent: 1 large egg, whisked gently
  • Hydration: Roughly 1 cup (250ml) room-temperature water
  • For Frying: 2 cups (500ml) neutral vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Whisk the Base: In a medium mixing bowl, combine your cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Stir in the whisked egg, then gradually incorporate the water. You are looking for a soft, pliable dough that holds its shape without being overly sticky.
  2. Shape the Festivals: Pinch off small, golf-ball-sized portions of the dough. Roll them between your palms to create the signature cigar or oval shape, roughly 4 to 5 inches long.
  3. The Deep Fry: Pour the oil into a heavy-bottomed pot and heat until it reaches a high temperature. Carefully lower a few pieces of dough into the hot oilâ€”do not overcrowd the pan. Fry for 3 to 4 minutes, turning them occasionally until the crust is a deep, uniform golden-brown.
  4. Drain and Serve: Transfer the festivals to a wire rack or paper towels to remove excess oil. Serve immediately while they still have that characteristic outer crunch.
