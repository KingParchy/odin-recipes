Jerk Chicken

Home

Description

Jerk Chicken is a world-renowned dish defined by its bold, fiery, and aromatic flavor profile. The process begins with a "jerk" marinade or dry rub, traditionally built on a foundation of crushed allspice berries (pimento) and Scotch bonnet peppers. These are blended with scallions, fresh thyme, ginger, garlic, and warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg to create a complex balance of heat, sweetness, and earthiness.

Ingredients

  • The Protein: 2 whole fresh chickens, divided into quarters.
  • Aromatic Base Paste
    • 1 tablespoon finely ground pimento (allspice)
    • 7 scallions, chopped into rounds
    • 3 Scotch Bonnet chilies (deseeded if preferred)
    • 3 plump garlic cloves, peeled
    • 4 sprigs of fresh thyme leaves
    • 5 fresh or 2 dried bay leaves, crushed
    • Kosher salt and cracked black pepper to taste
  • Savory Reduction Glaze
    • Reserved half-portion of the Base Paste
    • 3 tablespoons fresh ginger, finely grated
    • 3 cups (750ml) filtered water
    • Thickening slurry: 3 tablespoons cornstarch mixed with 3 tablespoons water

Steps

  1. Create the Paste: Combine the allspice, scallions, chilies, garlic, and herbs in a food processor. Blend the mixture until it reaches a uniform, pasty consistency, seasoning generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Marination: PJamaican lace the chicken pieces into a large resealable plastic bag. Work half of your prepared aromatic paste into the meat, ensuring every piece is well-coated. Seal the bag and allow it to marinate in the refrigerator—ideally overnight for maximum flavor depth.
  3. Slow Roasting: Arrange the seasoned chicken on a baking tray and place in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F). Bake slowly for approximately 1 hour until the juices run clear and the skin is darkened.
  4. Finishing the Glaze: Transfer the remaining half of the paste into a small saucepan over high heat. Stir in the grated ginger and water, bringing the liquid to a rolling boil. Dial the heat back to a simmer, whisk in the cornstarch slurry, and stir continuously until the sauce becomes glossy and thick.
